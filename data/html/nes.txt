A total of 717 known licensed game titles were released for the [[Nintendo Entertainment System]] [[video game console]] during its life span, 680 of these games released in North America (if you count ''Short Order'', ''Eggsplode!''. ''[[Back to the Future Part II & III|Back to the Future Part II]]'' and ''[[Back to the Future Part II & III|Back to the Future Part III]]'' as 4 separate games) plus 2 championship cartridges, with an additional 35 released in Europe or Australia. This list does not feature unlicensed NES games. The Nintendo Entertainment System (NES) — renamed from the Family Computer, or Famicom – was first released in North America on {{Start date|1985|10|18}}.<ref>{{cite book | first = Van | last = Burnham | year = 2001 | title = Supercade: A Visual History of the Videogame Age, 1971–1984 | page = 375 | publisher = MIT Press | location = Cambridge, Massachusetts | isbn = 0-262-52420-1 }}</ref> The NES was released in Europe and Australia in late 1985 and distributed by various third-party companies until [[Nintendo]] took over distribution in 1990.<ref name="burnham">{{cite book|first=Van |last=Burnham |year=2001 |title=Supercade: A Visual History of the Videogame Age, 1971–1984 |page=375 |publisher=MIT Press |location=Cambridge, Massachusetts |isbn=0-262-52420-1}}</ref> The final licensed NES game released was ''[[Wario's Woods]]'' on December 10, 1994 in North America and the PAL-exclusive ''[[The Lion King (video game)|The Lion King]]'' in 1995, and the most recent unlicensed game of significant notability released is ''Data East All Star Collection'' released in December 2017.<ref>https://www.retrogamer.net/blog_post/retro-bit-multi-carts-on-sale-this-weekend/</ref> The NES was succeeded by the [[Super Nintendo Entertainment System]], released in 1991.

==Licensed games==
There are a total of 715 known licensed game titles of which 678 were released in North America along with 2 championship games. 35 were exclusively released outside North America, fully listed below.
